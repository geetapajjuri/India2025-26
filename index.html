India 2025-26

A Journey Through Incredible India

Dec 25, 2025 – Jan 3, 2026

From the sacred hills of Tirupati to the golden sands of Jaisalmer, this photo journal captures ten unforgettable days exploring India's rich heritage, breathtaking landscapes, and warm hospitality. Join us as we relive the magic of ancient temples, royal palaces, and New Year's celebrations under the desert stars.

Tirupati Tirumala

A Spiritual Journey to the Abode of Lord Venkateswara

Our journey began with something truly sacred – the pilgrimage to Tirumala, one of the most revered temples in the world. Nestled in the Seshachalam Hills of Andhra Pradesh, this ancient temple dedicated to Lord Venkateswara draws millions of devotees every year, making it one of the most visited religious sites on Earth.

The most memorable part? Walking up the legendary Srivari Mettu – over 3,550 steps carved into the hillside, each one bringing us closer to the divine. As the morning mist cleared and the temple gopuram came into view, every aching muscle felt worth it. There's something magical about earning your darshan through sweat and devotion, the same way pilgrims have done for centuries.

The temple's wealth of history spans over a millennium, with inscriptions dating back to the 9th century. Today, the Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams manages this spiritual treasure, ensuring that the divine experience remains unchanged for generations to come.

Highlights

  • Climbed 3,550+ steps of Srivari Mettu
  • One of the richest temples in the world
  • Ancient history dating back to 9th century
  • Divine darshan of Lord Venkateswara

Delhi Gate

Where History Stands Tall

Our first glimpse of North India's Mughal grandeur came at Delhi Gate – a magnificent portal that has witnessed centuries of history unfold. Standing before these imposing walls, you can feel the weight of empires that rose and fell within these ancient grounds.

The intricate red sandstone architecture speaks of a time when every archway was crafted to impress, every detail designed to inspire awe. Walking through the gate feels like stepping through a time portal, leaving behind the modern world and entering an era of emperors and epic tales.

This is where our Rajasthan adventure truly began – a fitting introduction to the architectural wonders that awaited us in the days ahead.

Highlights

  • Historic Mughal architecture
  • Gateway to India's rich heritage
  • Stunning red sandstone craftsmanship
  • Perfect start to our North India journey

Taj Mahal & Agra

A Monument to Eternal Love

Nothing quite prepares you for that first glimpse of the Taj Mahal. As you pass through the main gate and the ivory-white marvel comes into view, time simply stops. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan as a mausoleum for his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is poetry in marble.

We also explored the lesser-known but equally fascinating areas around Agra, including the marble craftsmen's workshops near the moonlight zone – where the magic of "Bajrangi Bhaijaan" (2015) was captured. Remember that heartwarming scene where Salman Khan's character helps little Munni? Some of those beautiful moments were filmed right here among the skilled artisans who carry on the Mughal tradition of marble inlay work.

Standing at the marble dumpyard, watching craftsmen transform raw stone into intricate patterns, you realize that the Taj Mahal isn't just a monument – it's a living legacy of craftsmanship that continues to thrive in these very streets.

Highlights

  • One of the Seven Wonders of the World
  • Filming location for 'Bajrangi Bhaijaan' (2015)
  • Marble craftsmen keeping Mughal traditions alive
  • Built 1632-1653 by 20,000 artisans

Kishangarh

The Marble City of Rajasthan

Between Jaipur and Ajmer lies a hidden gem that most tourists overlook – Kishangarh, famous for its exquisite miniature paintings and stunning marble works. Known as the "Marble City of India," this charming town gave us a glimpse into Rajasthan's lesser-known artistic heritage.

The town is renowned for the Kishangarh school of painting, particularly the iconic "Bani Thani" – often called the "Indian Mona Lisa." The delicate brushwork and romantic themes of these miniatures tell stories of devotion and love that have captivated art lovers for centuries.

Driving through the marble markets, we witnessed artisans transforming raw stone into intricate sculptures and architectural elements. It's humbling to realize that the same craftsmanship traditions that built the Taj Mahal continue to thrive in workshops like these.

Highlights

  • Famous for Kishangarh miniature paintings
  • Home of the 'Bani Thani' - Indian Mona Lisa
  • Major marble crafting hub
  • Rich artistic heritage off the tourist trail

Jaipur & Amer Fort

The Pink City's Royal Splendor

Welcome to Jaipur, the Pink City – where every corner tells a story of Rajputana grandeur. But the crown jewel of our visit was undoubtedly the magnificent Amer Fort, perched majestically on Aravalli hills overlooking Maota Lake.

Walking through these palatial halls, you can almost hear the echoes of history. This is where the epic love story of "Jodhaa Akbar" (2008) was brought to life, with Aishwarya Rai and Hrithik Roshan dancing through these very corridors in the stunning "Khwaja Mere Khwaja" sequence. The Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace) with its intricate mirror work still takes your breath away, just as it did for Mughal emperors centuries ago.

Built by Raja Man Singh I in 1592, this fort represents the perfect blend of Hindu Rajput and Mughal architecture. The morning light filtering through the latticed windows, the cool marble underfoot, the stunning views of the pink city below – every moment here felt like stepping into a living movie set. And of course, we couldn't miss the iconic Jal Mahal – the Water Palace rising dreamlike from Man Sagar Lake!

Highlights

  • Filming location for 'Jodhaa Akbar' (2008)
  • Sheesh Mahal - Hall of Mirrors
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Jal Mahal - the floating Water Palace

Jodhpur - The Blue City

Where the Sky Meets the Streets

From a distance, Jodhpur looks like the sky has melted into the city. Spread beneath the magnificent Mehrangarh Fort, the old city is a mesmerizing maze of blue-washed houses that seem to glow in the desert sun. Why blue? Some say it was to denote Brahmin homes, others claim it keeps the houses cool – but whatever the reason, the effect is absolutely magical.

The Blue City has captured the imagination of filmmakers worldwide. From "The Dark Knight Rises" (2012) which featured the imposing Mehrangarh Fort as an exotic prison, to numerous Bollywood productions, these azure streets have provided the backdrop for countless stories.

Walking through the narrow lanes of the old city, with blue walls rising on either side and the massive fort looming above, feels like wandering through a painter's dream. The warm chai from street vendors, the friendly waves from locals peeking out of blue doorways, the call to prayer echoing across the rooftops – Jodhpur captures your heart in the most unexpected ways.

Highlights

  • Iconic blue-washed old city houses
  • Mehrangarh Fort featured in 'The Dark Knight Rises'
  • One of Rajasthan's most photogenic cities
  • Rich Marwari culture and cuisine

Jaisalmer - The Golden City

New Year's Under the Desert Stars

And then came Jaisalmer – the grand finale of our journey! Rising like a golden mirage from the Thar Desert, this ancient city welcomed us with open arms (and spectacular sand dunes) for an unforgettable New Year's celebration.

Our adventure began at the desert tent camps – and let me tell you, these aren't your regular camping tents! Think luxury "glamping" under a canopy of stars so bright you forget you're on Earth. The New Year's Eve dance party under the desert sky, with bonfires crackling and traditional Rajasthani music filling the air, was pure magic.

But the real thrills? The camel rides across the undulating dunes at sunset, the ATV adventures that kicked up golden sand clouds, and the jeep safari that bounced us across the desert terrain. Fair warning: the jeep ride was INCREDIBLE... but also a firm reminder that we're not as young as we used to be! My back definitely had some opinions the next day. Worth every bump though!

The sand dunes at Sam village, about 45 km from the city, offered the most surreal landscapes – watching the sun set over endless golden waves of sand while sitting atop a camel is an experience that belongs in everyone's bucket list.

Highlights

  • New Year's Eve celebration in the desert
  • Luxury tent camping under the stars
  • Camel rides, ATV & jeep safaris (memorable for all the right and wrong reasons!)
  • Sam Sand Dunes at sunset

Chennai

Journey's End in the Gateway to the South

Our incredible adventure came full circle as we returned to Chennai – the vibrant capital of Tamil Nadu and the gateway to South India. After ten days of exploring the wonders of North India, there's something comforting about being back in the warm embrace of the South.

Chennai, with its unique blend of ancient temples, colonial architecture, and modern development, reminded us that India's diversity is truly endless. From the spiritual heights of Tirupati to the golden sands of Jaisalmer, and now back to the Bay of Bengal – what a journey it has been!

As we wrapped up our trip, these final moments in Chennai gave us time to reflect on the incredible memories we'd made: the 3,550 steps we climbed, the palaces we explored, the New Year's we celebrated under desert stars, and yes, the bumpy jeep rides that reminded us to respect our backs! Until the next adventure...

Highlights

  • Gateway to South India
  • Rich cultural heritage
  • Perfect ending to an incredible journey
  • Time to reflect on 10 amazing days
